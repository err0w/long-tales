# Failures Log

A running log of moments when I missed the point. One entry per miss. The goal is to not repeat the same mistake across sessions.

## Format

Each entry:

### YYYY-MM-DD — short topic

**What happened**: One or two sentences on what I produced or recommended.

**What was wrong**: What Saurabh flagged. Be specific. Don't generalise the mistake into a platitude.

**Correction**: What the right answer was or would have been.

**Why I missed it**: Honest diagnosis. Context I lacked, pattern I leaned on, wrong assumption, bad prior, skimmed the brief, picked the obvious answer over the right one.

**Rule going forward**: A concrete rule that would have caught this. If there is no useful rule, say so rather than inventing one.

### 2026-04-18 — Label mockups missed on visual direction

**What happened**: Produced three label mockups in an HTML file (Literary Kraft, Dark Editorial, Village Minimalist) and asked Saurabh to pick between them. He picked none.

**What was wrong**: All three pulled from my own reference stack: Penguin Modern Classics, Muji and Aesop, natural wine labels. Western-editorial vocabulary. None of it had been validated against Saurabh's actual visual sense for Long Tales. When he said "I like none," the only sensible next step was to back up and build a reference pool together, not to make three more speculative mockups.

**Correction**: Before producing mockups, build a shared moodboard. Pinterest references, sourced by Saurabh. Mockups come after the reference pool is co-validated, not before.

**Why I missed it**: I read "brand context says editorial and literary" (which is in brand-context.md and directionally correct) as licence to pick specific references I found aligned. I mistook directional alignment for visual validation. I also wanted to show velocity. Producing three options fast felt like progress, but it skipped the step that was actually needed.

**Rule going forward**: When visual direction is thin or unvalidated, the first deliverable is a shared reference pool (moodboard, Pinterest saves, physical references), not mockups. Mockups come only after references have been chosen together.

### 2026-04-19 — Landing page v2 missed on two enforced rules

**What happened**: Produced `landing-page-v2.html` with a made-up village name ("MASALPUR") as the biggest typography in the hero, and a CSS-rendered jar mockup built from label fonts and the buffalo SVG.

**What was wrong**: Two separate misses. First, village name has no business on any customer-facing surface. Saurabh corrected this and expanded the rule: specific village names, specific district names (Karauli, Sawai Madhopur, Dholpur, Alwar), and the Meena community identifier are internal sourcing strategy. They do not go on the jar, the label, the pamphlet, the landing page, ads, or WhatsApp. Village and family names appear on the QR story page only. The jar photo Saurabh shared in the same message confirmed this directly — Long Tales is the hero, no village on the front. Second, I did not use the approved artwork in `Collaterals/label-production-print.html`/`.pdf`. I built a parallel jar mockup instead.

**Correction**: (1) Brand name (Long Tales) is the hero on all customer-facing surfaces. Village and family names live on the QR story page and nowhere else. Districts and community identifiers never leave the internal strategy files. (2) Use the approved files in `Collaterals/` as the visual anchor for any customer-facing output. Do not re-render packaging from parts.

**Why I missed it**: I followed a line in `brand-context.md` ("village name larger than brand name") that was stale, instead of trusting the jar photo Saurabh had just shared. I never opened `Collaterals/`. I prioritised producing a full page (velocity) over verifying against the approved artwork and the latest instructions.

**Rule going forward**: Before producing any customer-facing output, open the latest approved file in `Collaterals/` and use it as the visual reference, not the fonts and colours pulled from the brief. If the brief and the approved artwork disagree, the artwork wins; flag the brief for update before producing anything. No specific village name, district name, family name, or community identifier on any customer surface. Generic geography ("villages of Rajasthan") is the only sourcing phrase that crosses the line.
