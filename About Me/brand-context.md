# Brand Context — Long Tales

This is the working brand blueprint. The first draft pulls from the original Claude Chat. Some numbers and competitor data are directionally right but not yet verified. Anything marked [verify] should be checked before being used externally.

## Identity

Name: Long Tales
Tagline: Ghee with a story worth telling.
Domain preference: longtales.co [verify availability and buy]
Founded: 2026. Registered in Gurgaon. Front-label marker: "EST. 2026 · GURGAON".

## Internal vs customer-facing split

This file is internal strategy. Most of it does not go on customer surfaces.

Customer-facing surfaces are: the jar label, the pamphlet, the landing page, WhatsApp scripts, ads, and any other outward piece a buyer or passer-by sees. These carry the brand name, the generic geography ("villages of Rajasthan"), the locked copy, and the QR code. Nothing more about sourcing.

Internal-only (never on customer surfaces): specific village names (Masalpur, etc.), specific district names (Karauli, Sawai Madhopur, Dholpur, Alwar), the Meena community identifier, family names, sourcing economics, competitive positioning, anything from the "What Long Tales is not" list.

The single exception is the QR story page for a specific jar. That page, and only that page, carries the village name and the family name for the batch in that jar. The QR is the mechanism that keeps the provenance promise without putting it on the front of the brand.

Brand name (Long Tales) is the hero typographically on the jar and the website. Village and family names do not appear anywhere else.

## What Long Tales is

Buffalo desi ghee. Bilona method. Wood or cow-dung fire. Single-family source. Bought as finished ghee from Meena families in Rajasthan (Karauli, Sawai Madhopur, Dholpur, Alwar districts). Sold as it arrives. Nothing added, blended, or standardised.

Every jar carries the batch month, FSSAI No, MRP, net weight, and a QR code. The QR takes the user to a mini-site (on the main Long Tales website), where they see the village name, the family name, and the story of that particular jar. Village and family names appear on the QR story page only, not on the jar or anywhere else.

It is not a manufacturing business. It is a sourcing, curation, and storytelling brand.

## What Long Tales is not

Not a cow ghee brand.
Not a wellness brand.
Not Ayurvedic.
Not A2.
Not premium (the price reflects the work, not a positioning claim).
Not a quick-commerce play.
Not on Amazon, Flipkart, Blinkit, or Zepto.
Not a paid Instagram brand.
Not influencer-led.
Not funded.

## Voice

Plainspoken. Slightly literary. Never preachy.

Dry humour is welcome. Earnestness is not. Adjective stacks are not.

Brand name (Long Tales) is the hero on packaging and on the website. Village and family names do not appear on customer-facing surfaces. They live on the QR story page only. This is a rule, not a stylistic preference.

One-line beats. No paragraphs of adjectives. Specific nouns over abstract ones.

## Aesthetic

References that do not match: anything with mandala motifs, anything that uses gold gradient, anything that uses the word "experience" in headline copy, anything that looks like a Patanjali label.

## SKUs and pricing [verify final numbers]

500g glass jar with 70mm metal lug cap. MRP ₹1,500. The acquisition SKU. First buy after sampling.

1kg glass jar with 70mm metal lug cap. MRP ₹2,800. The retention SKU. Introduced after 3 to 4 reorders, upsold in person by the rider.

A 250g Diwali gifting SKU is a possible third addition by August 2026.

## Distribution model (Gurgaon only at launch)

Sampling: 20g ghee samples plus a printed pamphlet, distributed door to door in gated societies. Candidate societies include DLF phases, Sushant Lok, Golf Course Road, South City, Malibu Town. [Pick first three societies before pilot.]

Ordering: WhatsApp message to a single number.

Delivery: Initially done by Saurabh only using his car.

Payment: cash on delivery.

Reorder: WhatsApp.

Why gated societies. A 400-flat society is a closed network. If 10 households buy, the other 390 hear about it at the lift, the park, or the RWA group. The society is the distribution channel and the marketing channel at once.

## Sourcing model

Community-based, not contractual. The villages are home, not a supply base. Trust infrastructure is inherited from the Meena network, not built.

Scaling to a new village is one introduction from one elder, not a hire.

Source price reference: ₹1,100 per kg of finished ghee at the village [verify quarterly, this changes].

## Competitive position [directional, verify before publishing]

Funded D2C ghee brands are cow-focused (Two Brothers at the top end, Anveshan, Akshayakalpa, Country Delight in the middle). None of them sell premium buffalo ghee with single-village provenance.

Shahji is the only heritage buffalo ghee brand in the broader market, but does not carry single-village or batch-level traceability.

Saras (Rajasthan dairy cooperative) owns the "Rajasthan" word in mass-market ghee, but plays in the ₹500–800 per kg segment.

The space Long Tales occupies, premium plus buffalo plus village-direct, appears empty. This thesis needs to be retested as the category grows.

Country Delight buffalo ghee customers in Gurgaon are not competition. They are the warmest available prospect list.

Batch month.
FSSAI license number.

Back-panel ingredient line (regulated panel): "Buffalo milk fat. Contains milk. No additives, preservatives, or colour."
MRP, inclusive of all taxes.
Net weight.
Storage line: "Store in a cool, dry place."
Handling line (separate block on back panel, titled How to use): "Thickens in winter, softens in summer. Normal. No fridge. Room temperature. Clean, dry spoon only."
Country of origin: India.

## Locked copy (single source of truth for pamphlet and website)

Front sub-line under the Hindi product name: "Just ghee. Nothing else. / From villages of Rajasthan."
Uses line: "Tempering dals, finishing rice, spoon on khichdi, smear on rotis."
QR caption: "Scan for this jar's story."
Signature line / packer: "Packed by Long Tales, Gurgaon. Origin: India."
Best-before convention: "12 months" from packaging, written as plain words on the label.

## Visual system (locked at label)

Brand mark: Rijksmuseum public-domain buffalo woodcut (RP-P-1931-1459, CC0), cropped to silhouette only. This is the animal across all brand assets. No other buffalo illustration.
Palette: ink #1a1410 on kraft #e8d6ac. Accent red #8b3e2f used sparingly (ornaments, Hindi headings).
Type: Ultra (brand wordmark), Arvo bold (section heads, data), Lora italic (body and sub-lines), Inter (small caps, reg labels), Tiro Devanagari Hindi (Devanagari only).
Frame: a thin double border inset 2.5mm from the cut, kraft-dark on kraft-light. Used on all rectangular print pieces (labels, pamphlet cover, batch cards).
Brand name (Long Tales, Ultra) is the hero on packaging and on the website. Village and family names do not appear on customer-facing surfaces. They live on the QR story page only.

## Nutrition facts (500g pack)

Serving 5g. 100 servings per pack. 45 kcal per serving. Protein 0g. Carbohydrates 0g (Sugars 0g). Total Fat 5g (Saturated 3.2g, Trans 0g). Sodium 0mg. % RDA on a 2000 kcal diet, approximate.

## FSSAI status

License type: Basic Registration, Trade and Retail category.
Threshold: turnover up to ₹1.5 Cr per the 2026 rule, which leaves headroom for a long time.
Annual fee: ₹100.

Application status: in progress on foscos.fssai.gov.in.
