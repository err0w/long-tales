# Parked strategic decisions

Short notes on decisions that are directionally settled but not yet ready for their own strategy doc. Each item graduates to a dedicated file in `Strategy/` when it's time to operationalize.

## 1kg SKU introduction rule

Decision: the 1kg SKU is a retention SKU, not an acquisition SKU. The rider upsells it in person at delivery, starting on the third order from a given customer (i.e., after at least two prior orders). No aggregate trigger (no "30+ repeat customers" threshold). The rule is per-household.

Open question: whether 1kg is available on request from day one for customers who explicitly ask, or held back entirely until the rider upsell moment. This affects whether the website or pamphlet even lists 1kg as an option at launch.

## Seasonal pricing (winter batch)

Decision in principle: MRP flexes up in winter months, when ghee quality is at its peak. Direction to operationalize:

- Dec, Jan, Feb jars labelled "Winter batch" on the batch card.
- Premium of ₹150–200 per 500g jar over the standard year-round price.
- One line on the card explaining why (butterfat is highest when buffaloes are on winter feed).

The point is to turn a price increase into part of the story, not to hide it. When seasonal pricing is ready to ship, this note graduates to `Strategy/pricing/`.

## Diwali 250g as a marketing play

Decision: the 250g jar is not a core SKU. It's a Diwali-only gifting format, produced in small numbers and given (or sold at minimal margin) to high-value customers, society RWA contacts, and potential ambassadors.

Not a revenue SKU. No plan to list it on the website or pamphlet. Produced once a year around October, designed to be given, not ordered. Format and quantity to be decided when Diwali 2026 planning starts.
